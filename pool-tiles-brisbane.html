Tiled Pools Are Elegant and Easy to Maintain

Owning a pool is a luxury, and while a painted concrete pool is functional, a tiled pool adds an elegant finish, as well as making it considerably easier to maintain. Both ceramic and glass tiles are the two most commonly used tiles for pool interiors.

PERC Tiling are leading pool tiling contractors in Brisbane and bring over 5 years of reliable experience to both private home and institution pool owners. Our promise to you is high finishing standards and excellent customer care.

Choosing Your Pool Tiles

While there are quite a variety of pool tile options, your choice will be based on what look you are trying to achieve, and what budget you have for it. Depending on what you are prepared to spend, you can choose either glass or ceramic tiles, with the former being more expensive than the latter.

A pool that blends in with the surroundings creates an attractive aesthetic to the environment, and choosing the right tiles is necessary in helping to create a stunning effect.

The following is a brief look at what each option affords:

  • Glass Tiles

Glass tiles have become quite popular over the last few years due to the beautiful reflections they create underwater. The play on the change of water and light creates a beautiful, often inviting and mesmerising effect.

An advantage to glass tiles is that you can use the tiles in different colours to create a stunning effect. The quality of glass tile ranges from low to high, with the high creating greater optical quality.

  • Porcelain Tiles

Porcelain or ceramic tiles have been the standard tiles used for many swimming pools and are favoured because of their low cost and durability. They also come in a wide variety of choices and are very easy to maintain.

In addition, porcelain tiles can be customised by adding hand-painted designs, or by adding a textured look or even installed as mosaics.

  • Stone Tiles

Stone tiles are usually used for pool decks and patios and add an attractive natural look in addition to the textured feel of the stone.

Whatever your choice, a chat with a member of our team should help you decide what would be best for you.

Size Matters When it Comes to Pool Tiles

Whatever your choice of pool tile, the size will matter and choosing carefully should ensure you achieve your desired look. Large tiles tend to crack far more easily, with larger glass tiles being susceptible to thermal shock, while smaller glass tiles do tend to keep longer.
