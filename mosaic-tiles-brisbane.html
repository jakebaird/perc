Mosaic Tiles are Very Versatile

Mosaic tiling presents an opportunity to create a variety of outlooks, depending on the material you choose. Mosaic tiling can also be used in various different areas to create beautiful and creative designs that will give a unique look to any room in your home.

PERC Tiling have been providing mosaic tile supplies in Brisbane, for over 5 years, and have gained a reputation as experts in tiling services.

Various Types of Mosaic Tiles

Mosaic tiling comes in an assortment of sizes, colours and materials. The most common of these materials are porcelain, ceramic, glass, or stone. The term mosaic means an image or pattern produced by putting together small pieces of stone, tile, glass or other material.

Intricate and beautiful designs can either be hand-painted or etched and, by the use of modern-day water-jet technology, create beautiful seamless mosaics. Mosaics can be used to decorate various areas of residential or commercial properties, to greatly enhance the beauty of the area.

Where to Use Mosaic Tiles

Mosaic through the centuries has been used for various decorative reasons, starting from creating artefacts to decorating building structures. Dating back to Greek times, mosaic tiles have evolved with the passing times, and are still very much in use today.

They add style and dimension to any ordinary surface, to create stunning looks that catch the eye. At PERC Tiling, we have a wide variety of mosaic tiling options for you to choose from, that will be sure to enhance your surface. 

Mosaic tiles can be used in residential areas on surfaces such as the walls and floors of your kitchen, living room, bathroom, and pool spaces, and can even be extended to decorating seats, etc.

For commercial properties, mosaic tiles can be used to create a beautiful and tasteful facade on the floors and walls of entrance ways, offices, bathrooms, and pool areas.

Keeping Your Mosaic Tiles Clean

Mosaic tiles are made from different materials such as stone, glass, ceramic, and other materials, each of which has its own colour and texture. Whichever area you plan to lay mosaic tiles, keeping them clean ensures that they retain their beauty, and will reduce the chance of damage. Therefore, it’s important to learn how to look after them correctly.

  • Never use corrosive materials
  • Wipe down regularly with mild detergent and water
  • Use a non-abrasive sponge and not brittle brushes
  • Rinse off detergent with warm water and dry with a soft cloth
  • For floors, use a soft mop
  • Regularly vacuum
  • Mop up any spills
  • Don’t scratch.

Maintaining your mosaics is also important for your image. Unkempt and dirty mosaics will speak volumes to those around you.
